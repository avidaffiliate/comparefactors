Tribunal Risk Rating

Our headline rating for each factor is based entirely on their history with the Housing and Property Chamber‚ÄîScotland's tribunal for disputes between homeowners and factors. This provides an objective measure based on official adjudicated outcomes, not opinion.

The rating considers three key dimensions:

40%

Case Volume

How many tribunal cases has this factor faced, relative to the number of properties they manage? A factor managing 50,000 flats with 20 cases is performing better than one with 500 flats and 10 cases.

Score = 100 ‚àí (cases per 10,000 properties √ó 20)
35%

Case Severity

How serious were the issues in each case? We analyse each tribunal decision to assess the severity of code breaches, from minor administrative failures to serious misconduct.

Score = 100 ‚àí (average severity √ó 10)
25%

Recent Activity

What's happened in the last 3 years? Recent cases matter more than historical ones‚Äîa factor that cleaned up their act deserves credit, while recent problems are more relevant to today's homeowners.

Score = 100 ‚àí (recent cases per 10,000 √ó 10)
Composite Score Formula
Volume Score √ó 0.40 + Severity Score √ó 0.35 + Recency Score √ó 0.25 ‚àí PFEO Penalty
üìè Why "per 10,000 properties"?

Using rates rather than raw counts ensures fair comparison. Scotland's largest factors manage tens of thousands of properties, while smaller firms might manage just a few hundred. Comparing absolute case numbers would unfairly penalise larger operations.

Enforcement Order Penalties

A Property Factor Enforcement Order (PFEO) is the most serious sanction the tribunal can impose. It's a legally binding order requiring the factor to take specific action, and failure to comply can result in prosecution.

Our scoring applies additional penalties for PFEOs:

  • Unresolved PFEO + High Case Volume = RED
    Factors with an unresolved enforcement order AND 3 or more tribunal cases in 5 years are rated RED. This combination indicates both non-compliance and a pattern of tribunal activity.
  • Unresolved PFEO + Low Case Volume = ORANGE
    Factors with an unresolved enforcement order but fewer than 3 cases are rated ORANGE, not RED. The low case volume suggests this may be an isolated issue rather than systemic problems.
  • Multiple Resolved PFEOs = ORANGE Maximum
    Factors with two or more PFEOs in the past 5 years are capped at ORANGE rating, even if all orders have been resolved. A pattern of enforcement indicates systemic issues.
  • Score Penalty
    Each PFEO deducts 15 points from the composite score, up to a maximum penalty of 40 points. This affects the final score even when tier rules don't apply.

Rating Tiers Explained

The final rating tier is determined by applying rules in priority order. This ensures that serious issues (like unresolved enforcement orders) always surface, regardless of overall score.

Customer Reviews

We aggregate customer reviews from Google and Trustpilot where available. These are displayed separately from the tribunal rating because they measure different things‚Äîcustomer satisfaction vs. regulatory compliance.

Review scores are weighted by review count when calculating combined ratings. A factor with 200 Google reviews at 4.2 stars carries more statistical weight than one with 5 reviews at 4.8 stars.

‚ö†Ô∏è Reviews Have Limitations

Online reviews can be influenced by fake reviews, review solicitation practices, or selection bias (unhappy customers are more likely to leave reviews). We present this data for context but recommend focusing on the tribunal record for objective assessment.

Limitations & Caveats

We believe in transparency about what our methodology can and cannot tell you:

  • Property counts are estimates. For some factors, we don't have accurate property counts from FOI data. In these cases, we use a default estimate of 2,000 properties. This is flagged on affected profiles.
  • Severity scores use AI analysis. We use AI to extract and assess severity from tribunal decisions. While we've validated this against human review, some edge cases may be imperfectly scored.
  • Tribunal cases don't tell the whole story. A factor with zero tribunal cases isn't necessarily excellent‚Äîthey might just have homeowners who don't know their rights. Conversely, some cases are dismissed or involve frivolous complaints.
  • Data has a time lag. Tribunal decisions can take months to be published. Our data reflects published decisions, not ongoing cases.
  • We only cover registered factors. Unregistered factors operating illegally won't appear in our database at all.

Editorial Independence

Compare Factors Scotland is an independent service. Our ratings are calculated algorithmically from official data sources‚Äîno factor can pay for a better rating or to have negative information removed.

We may earn referral fees when homeowners request quotes through our platform. This commercial relationship is kept entirely separate from our scoring methodology. The factors we recommend in our "Get Quotes" service are chosen based on user location and factor availability, not payment.

If you believe any information on our site is inaccurate, please contact us with supporting evidence and we'll investigate promptly.
